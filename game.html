The game

A Flashcross game is a mixture of puzzles, cycling, and photography. The objective of the game is to cycle to various locations, and re-make photos that have been taken at those locations as accurately as possible.

A Flashcross game is played with teams consisting of two people. A typical game takes 4 hours, and has around 15 locations to re-make photos at. Visiting a location and re-making a photo successfully will give you points. The team who gets the most points within the allotted time, wins.

How does it work?

At the start of the game all teams are given a map with marked locations. They are also given as many assignments; one for each location on the map.

Each assignment consists of four photos: one main "location" photo and three bonus "puzzle" photos. When a team arrives at the location, the team should start with re-making the main photo. The main photo has to include one of the team members and both bicycles to prove that they've actually visited the location.

The puzzle photos are hidden within approximately 50 meters of where the photographer is standing while taking the main photo. If a team doesn't reproduce the main photo first, they will not get any points for the puzzle photos.

Each team can decide for themselves the route and in which order they want to visit the locations. It's not possible to visit all locations.

When a team arrives back at the start/finish location, they hand over their photos to the organizers, who then note down the return time and analyze the team's photos comparing them to the assignments. Once all teams have returned, the scores are calculated and the winners are announced.

Score calculation usually takes about an hour after last team finishes. Awarding takes place immediately after. Teams are allowed to take part in analyzing and defend their photos. All results, maps and photos would be published on the website.

What do you need to take part?

  • A bicycle
  • A friend with another bicycle
  • A camera (or a phone with a camera)
  • A bicycle lock (recommended)
  • To register here
  • Pay a small fee of 10 euro

How are photos judged?

In order for a photo to give any points, it needs to match the assignment photo as closely as possible. This means that the photo needs to be taken of the same spot, with the same angle, the same distance and of the same object. :)

If a photo does not match the above requirements, it will not be counted towards the final score.

There are two types of photos; subject photos and foreshortening photos. The type of photo is always marked on the assignment with either an "S" or an "F" in the top left corner.

Subject photos

You need to find and take a photo of an object. What exactly you have to get is obvious from the assignment photo. Foreshortening, cropping and a little shift of focus are not critical. The most important is to find the correct subject, the exact thing or view that is in the assignment photo (from several similar subjects).

Foreshortening photos

You need to study the assignment for its smallest details, pay attention to the foreground as well as to the background. The photo should be taken from the exact same spot and in the same direction. The composition should be the same as well. Shifting the borders a little bit will not affect it passing the criteria. Shift of focus from foreground to background doesn't matter.

How is the score calculated?

For each successfully re-made photo you will receive points. The amount of points depends on how many other teams have successfully made the same photo. The less teams have made a photo, the more points it is worth.

The points are allocated as follows:

Amount of teams who made the same photo Amount of points the photo is worth
1 team 10 points
2 teams 8 points
3 teams 5 points
from 3 to 20% of all teams 4 points
from 20-30% of all teams 3 points
from 30-50% of all teams 2 points
from 50-100% of all teams 1 point

Only teams which arrive no later than 50 minutes after the control finish time will be taken into account.

The amount of points is also affected by the time you arrive back at the finish. For each minute you are later than the control finish time, 2% is deducted from your total score.

Are there any camera requirements?

You can use whatever camera you want, as long as you are able to provide the photos digitally the moment you finish. We recommend using a smartphone or camera with SD card.

Please make sure the photos from your device can be transferred to a computer. If your phone cannot be connected to our computer, or we cannot read the photos from the camera, they don't count and the team is disqualified. Please note, that we would take all the photos from your device for analyzing. So take care not to have photos with private content. Clean your card before the event.

How should photos be submitted?

Photos must be submitted as soon as a team finishes. They must be submitted in one of the following ways.

Connecting your phone

We will have software ready to download photos from your phone to our computer. For Android phones we will use the regular Android File Transfer program. For iPhones we will use the regular OS X Image Capture program. Other types of phones are not supported. You are required to bring your own USB connector cable.

SD card

You can supply us with an SD card, a miniSD card, or a microSD card which contains the photos. This will enable you to use any camera which stores photos on an SD card.

Where does Flashcross come from?

Flashcross comes from Kharkiv, Ukraine. It was created by a group of friends who hated sitting on sofas but loved cycling, puzzles, and adventure.

The first event was organized in 2006 as a joke for their friends, with only 5 teams taking part. These days the event is hugely popular in Ukraine and Russia. There are about 1500 participants in 9 cities in Ukraine, Crimea, and Russia.

Flashcross.nl is the first attempt to bring the game outside its home region.
