The game

A Flashcross game is a mixture of puzzling, sport, and photography. The objective of the game is to cycle to various locations, and re-make photos that have been taken at those locations as accurately as possible.

A Flashcross game is played with teams consisting of two people. A typical game takes 4 hours, and will have 15 locations to re-make photos at. Re-making a photo successfully will give you points. The team who successfully re-made the most photos within the allotted time, wins.

How does it work?

At the start of the game all teams are given a map with 15 marked locations. They are also given 15 assignments; one for each location on the map.

Each assignment consists of four photos: one main photo and three bonus photos. If a team wants to get points for any of the photos at a location, they have to at least re-make the main photo. The main photo has to include one of the team members and both bicycles to prove that they've actually visited the location.

The bonus photos are always taken within 50 meters of where the main photo is taken. If a team doesn't first reproduce the main photo, they will not get any points for the bonus photos.

Each team can decide for themselves by which route and in which order they visit the locations. You don't have to visit all locations if you don't want to.

When a team arrives back at the start/finish location, they hand over their photos to the organizers, who then note down the return time and compare the photos to the assignments. Once all teams have returned, the scores are calculated and the winners are announced.

What do you need to take part?

You need at least the following:

  • To register
  • A bicycle
  • A friend with another bicycle
  • A camera (or a phone with a camera)
  • A bicycle lock (recommended)

How are photos judged?

In order for a photo to give any points, it needs to match the assignment photo as closely as possible. This means that the photo needs to be taken of the same object, from the same direction, with the same angle, and the same distance.

If a photo does not match the above requirements, it will not be counted towards the final score.

There are two types of photos; subject photos and foreshortening photos. The type of photo is always marked on the assignment with either an "S" or an "F."

Subject photos

You need to find and take a photo of an object. What exactly you have to get is obvious from looking at the assignment photo. Foreshortening, cropping and a little shift of focus are not critical. The most important is to find the correct subject, the exact thing or view that is in the assignment photo.

How is the score calculated?

For each successfully re-made photo you will receive points. The amount of points depends on how many other teams have successfully reproduced that photo. The less teams have reproduced a photo, the more points it is worth.

The exact formula is as follows: X ÷ Y

Where X is the total number of teams, and Y the number of teams who have successfully re-made that photograph. So if 10 teams participate, and 10 teams re-make a photograph, they each get 1 point. If instead 5 teams would re-make that photograph, they would each receive 2 points. If one team would re-make the photograph, they would receive 10 points.

The amount of points is also affected by the time you arrive back at the finish. For each minute you are late, 2% is deducted from your total score.

Are there any camera requirements?

You can use whatever camera you want, as long as you are able to provide the photos digitally the moment you finish. We recommend using a smartphone or camera with SD card.

How should photos be submitted?

Photos must be submitted as soon as a team finishes. They must be submitted in one of the following ways.

Connecting your phone

We will have software ready to download photos from your phone to our computer. For Android phones we will use the regular Android File Transfer program. For iPhones we will use the regular OS X Image Capture program. Other types of phones are not supported. You are required to bring your own USB connector cable. If your phone cannot be connected to the computer, or we cannot read the photos from the camera, they don't count.

SD card

You can supply us with an SD card, a miniSD card, or a microSD card which contains the photos. This will enable you to use any camera which stores photos on an SD card.

Where does Flashcross come from?

Flashcross comes from Kharkiv, Ukraine. It was created by a group of friends who hated sitting on sofas but loved cycling, puzzling, and adventure.

The first event was organized in 2007 as a joke for their friends, with only 5 teams taking part. These days the event is hugely popular in Ukraine and Russia. There are about 1500 participants in 9 cities in Ukraine, Crimea, and Russia.

Flashcross.nl is the first attempt to bring the game outside its home region of Ukraine.
